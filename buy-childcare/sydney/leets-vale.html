Buying a Childcare Business in Leets Vale, Sydney

Buying a childcare business in Leets Vale may be competitive, but this is because childcare in this suburb is a highly lucrative and low risk business. Childcare is an attractive business to be in, but there is one other factor driving demand that we will address. But firstly, you need to be aware of some of the laws and regulations.

Laws and Regulations for buying a childcare business in Hills District

In order to buy and run a childcare business in the Hills District, you will need to obtain compliance with the following regulations and standards:

  • Worker’s Compensation Insurance
  • Police Check
  • Working With Children Check
  • First Aid Certificate
  • Analphylaxis certificate
  • Provider Approval
  • Supply Approval
  • Public Liability Insurance
  • Professional Indemnity Insurance
  • National Quality Framework

The largest item on this list is the National Quality Framework which lays out standards for early childcare education nationwide. If you are familiar with this framework then you will be able to tell whether the businesses you are considering buying are in compliance. If they aren’t then you can estimate how much time and money it will cost to bring them into compliance.

What’s driving the demand for childcare businesses in Leets Vale?

As you may know, the childcare business market in the Hills District is booming. There are two main factors driving this, and the first is that the childcare industry is very profitable, and relatively low risk. Childcare is in high demand, it’s always in rising demand, and this demand is extremely stable even during the worst times of economic volatility. Furthermore, in the Sydney childcare market, new entrants (new childcare centres opening in the area) are quite rare, which we will talk about next.

The second factor relates to Sydney’s monumental property prices and scarcity of available land. This has presented a huge barrier to new childcare centres opening anywhere in the Hills District. The high upfront costs coupled with the risk of being a new entrant in addition to the scarcity of large blocks of land that are appropriately zoned and also not in areas of heavy business and manufacturing… causes opening a new childcare center to be very hard. And to make matters even harder, the approval process for a new childcare business has a notoriously long wait time.

These two factors both create a market where existing childcare businesses are in high demand. This means that when buying a childcare business in Leets Vale, you will face strong competition from other buyers, and will need to be at the top of your game.
