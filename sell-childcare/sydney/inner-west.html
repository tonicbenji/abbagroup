Selling Your Childcare Business in the Inner West

Selling a childcare business in the Inner West is a bit overwhelming due to two large factors. On the one hand you have the Inner West’s childcare oversupply, and on the other you have quite heavy regulations. So with regards to these obstacles, how can you sell your childcare business for the best price in the Inner West?

The Inner West Childcare Regulations

The Inner West childcare sector is an extremely regulated one, and as you know, these affect the profitability of your business (and hence the sale price). For instance, in 2018 the Federal Government’s Childcare Rebate was replaced with a Childcare Subsidy which increased payments to families, and hence increased the demand for childcare services.

Regulations like those will have a positive effect on the sale of your business, however other regulations can be detrimental. For instance, each local government, local area, street and property will have its own planning regulations. Is your land still zoned to allow a childcare development? Is your area zoned to allow high density buildings or industry? Factors like these will affect the direction that you take in selling your childcare business. For instance, would you consider selling your land to a non-childcare business?

The Inner West Childcare Oversupply – How To Beat It

As you are probably well aware, the Inner West does have an oversupply of childcare businesses. We mustn’t overstate this oversupply however, because the media does have a habit of exaggerating or generalising things like this. The truth is that this oversupply is worse in some areas than others. As a rule of thumb, densely populated areas have a greater oversupply because they have more families wanting childcare versus less land on which to provide it.

What do these supply dynamics mean when selling your the Inner West childcare business?

It means that when selling your childcare business in an oversupplied area, it will be harder to get a good price for your business. But, that doesn’t necessarily mean that you won’t get a good price. There are several ways that a good business broker will use in order to handle this exact situation. One example of these is selling to a competitor. Another is to use future projected growth figures to make a strong proposition.

As you can tell from the factors brushed upon in this article, there are many specifics to your personal business situation that still need to be considered in more depth. Furthermore, there are complexities to the market and to the legal-regulatory aspects of the sale that only an experienced business broker will know.
