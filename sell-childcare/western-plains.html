Selling a Childcare Business in the Western Plains

If you are looking to sell your business now in the Western Plains, you are selling at the right time. The supply of childcare businesses is limited, and the demand is extremely strong. However, you will want to maximise this large and very important opportunity, so there are few things that you must know.

The Western Plains Childcare Business Market

The current market for childcare businesses in the Western Plains is very opportune. Just recently in June 2018, the Australian Government announced that they are extending the childcare subsidy to children as young as three years. This has already surged the demand for childcare. And, the other big story you may have been hearing is the shortage in childcare placements. These and other factors are contributing to a strong market for selling a childcare business in the Western Plains.

To understand the market, firstly we’ll put you in the shoes of the buyer of a childcare business in the Western Plains. The buyer is looking for a childcare business that satisfies all their requirements and is local to where they live. However, there are not many childcare businesses going up for sale near them. The buyer can’t afford to start their own childcare business, because the upfront costs are high, so they are limited to the limited businesses that are on offer. When you list your childcare business for sale, the buyer’s heart leaps – they have found what they are looking for… but so have many other buyers. There is steep competition, and the buyer is willing to pay a high price to get the business that they are looking for.

Considering that this is a seller’s market, it should be easy to sell your childcare business. However, you don’t just want to sell your business flippantly, you want to maximise this opportunity to get a large return by selling at this rare and lucky time.

Tips For Selling Your Childcare Business In This Current Market

As business brokers who have extensive experience in the childcare sector, we can offer some tips that should improve the amount that you get from your business sale.

The first and most obvious tip is to avoid letting your customers hear about the sale. You don’t want customers to start switching to new childcare businesses before you sell. If this happens, then your prospective buyers will look at your accounting records and see a downturn in sales right before you plan to sell. This could raise some undue doubts.

Secondly, you must put substantial effort and some funding into marketing and listing your business. There are many avenues that you can take in marketing your business, but in the end the best options are a matter of professional opinion. For your specific business, certain printed, online and network marketing channels will be preferable. Furthermore, there is a whole field of written, graphic and sales marketing techniques that can help your business appeal to customers. For instance, what should be the main image of your childcare business – an empty property or a childcare center with children playing and having fun? Most marketers would say the second option.

Finally, the most important aspect of your sale is the asking price. This figure is the origin from which the entire selling and negotiation process is projected. This single number should be the product of extensive research, market knowledge, market analysis and strategy. You should think about what you think your business is worth, and what you expect to get from it, then you should talk to a business broker who has the necessary experience.
